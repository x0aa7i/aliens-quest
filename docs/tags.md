## Tag System

Tags classify solutions to the Fermi Paradox along a few orthogonal dimensions.
Each tag represents a **distinct explanatory lens**, allowing articles to overlap and be linked through shared characteristics.

A solution typically receives **3–5 tags**.

---

## Tag Categories

### 1. Driver

What primarily causes the silence.

- **sociological**
  The explanation depends on alien psychology, culture, ethics, or strategic choices.
  Examples: civilizations hiding, refusing contact, observing us.

- **physical**
  The explanation arises from hard constraints of physics, energy, scale, or technology.
  Examples: signal attenuation, interstellar travel limits, cosmic distances.

---

### 2. Outlook

How the scenario affects humanity.

- **optimistic**
  Relatively favorable implications for humanity.
  Civilizations may exist but are benign, distant, or simply not interacting yet.

- **pessimistic**
  Unfavorable implications.
  Scenarios may involve extinction risks, hostile civilizations, or humanity being truly alone.

---

### 3. Time Dependence

How strongly the explanation relies on timing.

- **temporal**
  The hypothesis depends on timing effects such as:
  - civilizations appearing at different eras
  - short technological windows
  - delayed expansion
  - cosmic timing mismatches.

- **rare**
  Intelligent or technological life is extremely uncommon due to major evolutionary barriers.

---

### 4. Detectability

Why civilizations are not observed.

- **hiding**
  Advanced civilizations deliberately avoid detection or contact.

- **invisible**
  Civilizations may exist but are undetectable due to physical or technological limitations.

---

### 5. Epistemic Basis

How strongly the explanation is grounded in science.

- **scientific**
  Based on mainstream scientific reasoning or established theory.

- **speculative**
  Requires significant assumptions or extrapolation beyond current evidence.

- **philosophical**
  Primarily conceptual or logical arguments rather than empirical models.

---

## Tagging Guidelines

- Assign **3–5 tags per solution**.
- Prefer **broad explanatory traits**, not specific details.
- Avoid assigning multiple tags from the same category unless clearly justified.
- Tags should help connect related solutions across different mechanisms.

The goal is to create **overlapping clusters of explanations**, making it easier to explore different answers to the Fermi paradox.
